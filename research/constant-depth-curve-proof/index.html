Constant Depth Curve: mathematical proof

Deriving the CDC bonding curve from first principles: the uniform-depth constraint reduces to a finite-difference equation with a log-affine solution, closed-form reserves, and an invariant.

By Pablo Baiocchi · cdc · market-making · bonding-curve · mathematics

Companion to PNK/USD market maker: model & path-independence validation.

Setup

Consider a bonding curve on a CLOB with token holdings x(P)x(P)x(P) and USD holdings y(P)y(P)y(P) as functions of price PPP, operating over a bounded range [Pa,Pb][P_a, P_b][Pa​,Pb​].

Uniform depth constraint: Define depth over a symmetric log-price band of half-width h>0h > 0h>0. At any price PPP such that both Pe−hPe^{-h}Pe−h and PehPe^{h}Peh lie within [Pa,Pb][P_a, P_b][Pa​,Pb​], the USD available on each side must equal a constant DDD:

y(P)−y(Pe−h)=D(bid depth)y(P) - y(Pe^{-h}) = D \quad \text{(bid depth)}y(P)−y(Pe−h)=D(bid depth) y(Peh)−y(P)=D(ask depth)y(Pe^{h}) - y(P) = D \quad \text{(ask depth)}y(Peh)−y(P)=D(ask depth)

The effective domain for the constraint is P∈[Paeh,Pbe−h]P \in [P_a e^{h}, P_b e^{-h}]P∈[Pa​eh,Pb​e−h]. Outside this interior band, the full symmetric log-band is no longer contained in [Pa,Pb][P_a, P_b][Pa​,Pb​], so the available depth on the truncated side is strictly less than DDD.

Note: if the band is instead defined arithmetically as P(1±f)P(1\pm f)P(1±f), the bid and ask sides involve different log-widths (ln⁡(1/(1−f))≠ln⁡(1+f)\ln(1/(1-f)) \neq \ln(1+f)ln(1/(1−f))=ln(1+f)), making exact two-sided uniformity impossible with a single curve. The log-price formulation avoids this asymmetry.

1. The functional equation

Substituting u=ln⁡Pu = \ln Pu=lnP and Y(u)=y(eu)Y(u) = y(e^u)Y(u)=y(eu), both constraints reduce to:

Y(u)−Y(u−h)=D∀ u∈[ln⁡Pa+h,  ln⁡Pb−h]Y(u) - Y(u - h) = D \quad \forall\, u \in [\ln P_a + h,\; \ln P_b - h]Y(u)−Y(u−h)=D∀u∈[lnPa​+h,lnPb​−h]

This is a finite-difference equation: YYY has constant increments over equal steps hhh in log-price space.

2. Solving the functional equation

The general smooth solution is:

Y(u)=Dh⋅u+ϕ(u)Y(u) = \frac{D}{h} \cdot u + \phi(u)Y(u)=hD​⋅u+ϕ(u)

where ϕ\phiϕ is any smooth hhh-periodic function (ϕ(u)=ϕ(u−h)\phi(u) = \phi(u - h)ϕ(u)=ϕ(u−h)), since:

Y(u)−Y(u−h)=Dh⋅h+ϕ(u)−ϕ(u−h)=DY(u) - Y(u-h) = \frac{D}{h} \cdot h + \phi(u) - \phi(u-h) = DY(u)−Y(u−h)=hD​⋅h+ϕ(u)−ϕ(u−h)=D

To obtain a homogeneous curve with no oscillatory structure in log-price, we select the canonical non-oscillatory solution ϕ=const\phi = \text{const}ϕ=const. This is the scale-invariant choice: the curve has constant USD depth per unit log-price, with no oscillatory structure or preferred frequencies in log-price space.

Note: monotonicity of y(P)y(P)y(P) alone is insufficient to force ϕ=const\phi = \text{const}ϕ=const. A small-amplitude periodic ϕ\phiϕ (e.g., ϕ(u)=εsin⁡(2πu/h)\phi(u) = \varepsilon \sin(2\pi u / h)ϕ(u)=εsin(2πu/h) with ε<D/2π\varepsilon < D/2\piε<D/2π) preserves Y′(u)>0Y'(u) > 0Y′(u)>0 everywhere while introducing oscillatory microstructure. Such solutions are mathematically valid but economically unnatural — they would create a curve where the marginal selling rate oscillates with price, with no economic justification for the preferred frequency. We exclude them by construction.

Thus:

Y(u)=Dh⋅u+constY(u) = \frac{D}{h} \cdot u + \text{const}Y(u)=hD​⋅u+const

Substituting back and setting y(Pa)=0y(P_a) = 0y(Pa​)=0 (boundary condition: all capital is in tokens at the lower bound):

 y(P)=C⋅ln⁡(P/Pa) \boxed{\,y(P) = C \cdot \ln(P / P_a)\,}y(P)=C⋅ln(P/Pa​)​

where:

C=DhC = \frac{D}{h}C=hD​

3. Deriving x(P)

Along a differentiable bonding curve with marginal price PPP, trade accounting gives:

dy=−P⋅dxdy = -P \cdot dxdy=−P⋅dx

(When price rises by dPdPdP, the curve sells dxdxdx tokens at price PPP, receiving P⋅dxP \cdot dxP⋅dx in USD.)

From y(P)=Cln⁡(P/Pa)y(P) = C \ln(P/P_a)y(P)=Cln(P/Pa​):

dydP=CP\frac{dy}{dP} = \frac{C}{P}dPdy​=PC​

Therefore:

dxdP=−1PdydP=−CP2\frac{dx}{dP} = -\frac{1}{P}\frac{dy}{dP} = -\frac{C}{P^2}dPdx​=−P1​dPdy​=−P2C​

Integrating with boundary condition x(Pb)=0x(P_b) = 0x(Pb​)=0 (all capital is in USD at the upper bound):

 x(P)=C(1P−1Pb) \boxed{\,x(P) = C\left(\frac{1}{P} - \frac{1}{P_b}\right)\,}x(P)=C(P1​−Pb​1​)​

4. The invariant

From x(P)x(P)x(P) and y(P)y(P)y(P):

x+CPb=CP⇒P=Cx+C/Pbx + \frac{C}{P_b} = \frac{C}{P} \quad \Rightarrow \quad P = \frac{C}{x + C/P_b}x+Pb​C​=PC​⇒P=x+C/Pb​C​

And:

ey/C=PPae^{y/C} = \frac{P}{P_a}ey/C=Pa​P​

Combining:

 (x+CPb)⋅ey/C=CPa \boxed{\,\left(x + \frac{C}{P_b}\right) \cdot e^{y/C} = \frac{C}{P_a}\,}(x+Pb​C​)⋅ey/C=Pa​C​​

This defines the state manifold of the curve. Infinitesimal trades respecting dy=−P⋅dxdy = -P \cdot dxdy=−P⋅dx preserve this invariant.

5. Verification of uniform depth

Bid depth at price PPP (USD spent as price falls from PPP to Pe−hPe^{-h}Pe−h):

Dbid(P)=y(P)−y(Pe−h)=Cln⁡PPa−Cln⁡Pe−hPa=C⋅h=D  ✓D_{bid}(P) = y(P) - y(Pe^{-h}) = C\ln\frac{P}{P_a} - C\ln\frac{Pe^{-h}}{P_a} = C \cdot h = D \;\checkmarkDbid​(P)=y(P)−y(Pe−h)=ClnPa​P​−ClnPa​Pe−h​=C⋅h=D✓

Ask depth at price PPP (USD received as price rises from PPP to PehPe^{h}Peh):

Dask(P)=y(Peh)−y(P)=Cln⁡PehPa−Cln⁡PPa=C⋅h=D  ✓D_{ask}(P) = y(Pe^{h}) - y(P) = C\ln\frac{Pe^{h}}{P_a} - C\ln\frac{P}{P_a} = C \cdot h = D \;\checkmarkDask​(P)=y(Peh)−y(P)=ClnPa​Peh​−ClnPa​P​=C⋅h=D✓

Both sides are exactly DDD, for all PPP in the effective domain [Paeh,Pbe−h][P_a e^{h}, P_b e^{-h}][Pa​eh,Pb​e−h].

6. Derived properties

Portfolio value:

V(P)=x(P)⋅P+y(P)=C(1−PPb+ln⁡PPa)V(P) = x(P) \cdot P + y(P) = C\left(1 - \frac{P}{P_b} + \ln\frac{P}{P_a}\right)V(P)=x(P)⋅P+y(P)=C(1−Pb​P​+lnPa​P​)

Range boundaries from capital. Given XXX tokens and YYY USD at current price P0P_0P0​, with CCC held fixed (i.e., target depth DDD and band half-width hhh fixed):

  • USD sets the downside: Pa=P0⋅exp⁡(−Y/C)P_a = P_0 \cdot \exp(-Y/C)Pa​=P0​⋅exp(−Y/C), which is always positive.
  • Tokens set the upside: Pb=C⋅P0/(C−X⋅P0)P_b = C \cdot P_0 / (C - X \cdot P_0)Pb​=C⋅P0​/(C−X⋅P0​). This requires X⋅P0<CX \cdot P_0 < CX⋅P0​<C for a finite positive PbP_bPb​. If X⋅P0=CX \cdot P_0 = CX⋅P0​=C, then Pb=∞P_b = \inftyPb​=∞. If X⋅P0>CX \cdot P_0 > CX⋅P0​>C, no finite or infinite positive upper bound exists within this curve family for that CCC.

These are independent — adding USD extends the downside without affecting the upside, and vice versa.

CLOB grid order size. On a log-uniform grid with spacing δ\deltaδ, each order is approximately C⋅δC \cdot \deltaC⋅δ USD, uniform at every price level.

7. Relationship to arithmetic percentage bands

If the depth band is defined arithmetically as [P(1−f),P(1+f)][P(1-f), P(1+f)][P(1−f),P(1+f)] instead of [Pe−h,Peh][Pe^{-h}, Pe^{h}][Pe−h,Peh], the bid and ask log-widths differ:

  • Bid: hbid=ln⁡(1/(1−f))h_{bid} = \ln(1/(1-f))hbid​=ln(1/(1−f))
  • Ask: hask=ln⁡(1+f)h_{ask} = \ln(1+f)hask​=ln(1+f)

No single log-affine curve can produce exact constant depth on both sides simultaneously. The relative excess of bid depth over ask depth is:

DbidDask−1=ln⁡(1/(1−f))ln⁡(1+f)−1=f+O(f2)\frac{D_{bid}}{D_{ask}} - 1 = \frac{\ln(1/(1-f))}{\ln(1+f)} - 1 = f + O(f^2)Dask​Dbid​​−1=ln(1+f)ln(1/(1−f))​−1=f+O(f2)

At f=0.02f = 0.02f=0.02, bid depth exceeds ask depth by ~2%, which is negligible in practice. But exact two-sided uniformity under arithmetic bands is impossible with this curve family.
