15:T23a9,<p><strong>TL;DR — DAMM is 6.2x more capital efficient than Competitor</strong> on the WETH/[TOKEN] pool (Base, Aerodrome Slipstream, 0.3% fee tier) over a 28-day window.</p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> for this case study, one of the tokens in the pair has been redacted and replaced with &quot;TOKEN&quot;, and the competing market maker is unnamed, in honor of client confidentiality.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>Capital efficiency in numbers:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>6.2x</strong> more fees per ETH deployed (DAMM vs Competitor)</li>
<li><strong>0.28 ETH</strong> to capture 1% of pool fees (DAMM) vs <strong>1.73 ETH</strong> (Competitor)</li>
<li>DAMM deploys only <strong>15% of total capital</strong> as active liquidity, keeping 85% in reserve — yet matches Competitor&#39;s fee APY on total capital with <strong>18x less total capital</strong></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Why the ETH difference?</strong> DAMM deploys 1.19 ETH on average; Competitor deploys 141.35 ETH — 119x more. Despite this, DAMM captures 4.2% of all pool fees vs Competitor&#39;s 81.7%. Per ETH deployed, DAMM earns 6.2x more fees (38.6% vs 6.3% fee APY).</p>
<p>The gap comes down to <strong>risk exposure</strong>: Competitor spreads capital across ~25,000 ticks with wide, static positions rebalanced every two weeks. DAMM concentrates into ~3,000 ticks centered on the current price, rebalanced daily. When [TOKEN] dropped 11.3% against WETH, Competitor&#39;s wide exposure meant higher absolute impermanent loss (−9.83 ETH vs −0.074 ETH), and their fees only recovered 6.9% of that loss. DAMM&#39;s fees recovered 47.6% of its IL — meaning DAMM&#39;s net cost of market making was −3.25% on deployed capital vs Competitor&#39;s −6.47%, <strong>making Competitor twice as expensive, with higher risk</strong>.</p>
<p><strong>Strategy structure:</strong> DAMM runs 2 concentrated positions (3,000 ticks total, ~±1,500 from current price) rebalanced daily to track price. Competitor runs 2 wide positions (~25,000 ticks total) rebalanced roughly biweekly. DAMM&#39;s positions are symmetric and centered; Competitor&#39;s are asymmetric and static.</p>
<p><strong>Pool:</strong> WETH/[TOKEN] Aerodrome Slipstream (Base) — 0.3% fee tier · <strong>Period:</strong> 28-day window · <strong>Swaps analyzed:</strong> 7,527</p>

<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>In a month where [TOKEN] fell 11.3% against WETH, DAMM&#39;s net cost of market making was <strong>−3.25% on deployed capital</strong>. Competitor&#39;s was <strong>−6.47%</strong> — twice as expensive.</p>
<p>Three facts drive this:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>DAMM&#39;s fees offset half its IL.</strong> Fee income covered 47.6% of impermanent loss, vs only 6.9% for Competitor. Concentrated liquidity earns proportionally more fees per unit of capital exposed.</li>
<li><strong>DAMM&#39;s IL was lower as a percentage.</strong> Tighter positions with daily rebalancing produced 6.21% IL vs Competitor&#39;s 6.95% — the active tracking limits exposure during sustained directional moves.</li>
<li><strong>DAMM does this with 119x less capital.</strong> The absolute cost of DAMM&#39;s market making was 0.039 ETH. Competitor spent 9.15 ETH to provide liquidity in the same pool, in the same month, with a worse net outcome per dollar.</li>
</ol>
<p>For a token issuer comparing MM providers: DAMM delivers the same depth per dollar at a fraction of the cost, loses less during adverse price moves, and recovers more of those losses through fees.</p>
<h2>Methodology</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Fee attribution:</strong> For each of 7,527 swaps, we reconstruct both LPs&#39; active liquidity at the swap tick from on-chain mint/burn events, then compute pro-rata fee share as <code>LP_liquidity / pool_total_liquidity</code>.</li>
<li><strong>Net P&amp;L:</strong> Position values computed at window boundaries using Uniswap V3 liquidity math (<code>getAmount0</code>/<code>getAmount1</code>), validated against on-chain mint amounts (&lt;2% error). IL = (end_value − start_value) − net_deposits. Net P&amp;L = IL + fees.</li>
<li><strong>Position attribution:</strong> NFPM Transfer event logs via <code>eth_getLogs</code> RPC, matching tx_hashes against pool mint/burn events.</li>
<li><strong>Capital:</strong> Average WETH + [TOKEN] deployed per rebalance, converted at median [TOKEN]/WETH price.</li>
<li><strong>APY:</strong> <code>(metric / capital_ETH) × (365 / 28) × 100</code></li>
</ul>
