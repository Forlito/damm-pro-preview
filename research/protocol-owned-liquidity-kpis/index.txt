15:Tb305,<h2>Liquidity Depth Efficiency (±1% band)</h2>
<p><strong>Purpose.</strong> The principal objective of protocol-owned liquidity (POL) is to let end-users execute modest trades with minimal price impact — i.e., <em>low slippage</em>.</p>
<p>Near-depth efficiency quantifies how much of the treasury&#39;s deployed capital is positioned exactly where those small trades occur, within ±1% of the current mid-price. A higher value means tighter spreads and a deeper market for everyday users.</p>

<h3>Interpretation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Numerator</strong> — aggregate, immediately-usable depth that cushions user orders up to a 1% move in either direction.</li>
<li><strong>Denominator</strong> — treasury capital at work.</li>
</ul>
<p>The ratio therefore answers: <em>&quot;How many dollars of true, near-mid liquidity do we supply for every dollar we&#39;ve deployed?&quot;</em></p>
<p>A rising <span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mtext>LDE</mtext><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">%</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\text{LDE}_{1\%}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.9024em;vertical-align:-0.2191em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">LDE</span></span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3448em;"><span style="top:-2.5198em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1%</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2191em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> signals higher capital productivity and lower expected slippage for retail-sized trades.</p>

<p>This methodology delivers a clear, statistically robust indicator of how effectively POL capital serves its core mandate: <strong>providing deep, low-slippage execution for end-users.</strong></p>
<h2>Capital Retention Ratio (CRR)</h2>
<p>This indicator describes how well <em>protocol-owned liquidity capital</em> retains value relative to a passive &quot;hold-both-assets&quot; position established at the moment of initial deployment.</p>

<h3>Interpretation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>CRR = 1.00</strong> — POL capital matches the passive hold; impermanent loss is zero.</li>
<li><strong>CRR &lt; 1.00</strong> — Capital has under-performed the passive hold; the gap is economic drag from impermanent loss.</li>
<li>Values trending closer to 1.00 confirm that inventory management, range selection, and rebalance cadence are effectively containing drift.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Measurement protocol</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Snapshot cadence</strong> — Capture pool reserves, lending balances, liquid token amounts, and current market prices at a predetermined daily interval.</li>
<li><strong>POL valuation</strong> — Convert every component to USD (or chosen base unit) to calculate <span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>V</mi><mtext>POL</mtext></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V_{\text{POL}}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8333em;vertical-align:-0.15em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.2222em;">V</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3283em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:-0.2222em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord text mtight"><span class="mord mtight">POL</span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.15em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span>.</li>
<li><strong>Passive-hold baseline</strong> — Multiply the <em>original</em> token quantities (recorded at initial deployment) by the same current prices to compute <span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>V</mi><mtext>HODL</mtext></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V_{\text{HODL}}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.8333em;vertical-align:-0.15em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right:0.2222em;">V</span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3283em;"><span style="top:-2.55em;margin-left:-0.2222em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord text mtight"><span class="mord mtight">HODL</span></span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.15em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span>. This baseline remains constant in quantity across all measurement windows, ensuring a consistent comparative.</li>
<li><strong>Publishing</strong> — Compute CRR for every snapshot. Report a 30-day rolling <strong>median</strong> to mute short-lived market noise while still flagging persistent drift.</li>
</ol>
<p>CRR provides an unambiguous gauge of capital preservation. Maintaining <strong>CRR</strong> near unity demonstrates that the POL engine is minimizing impermanent loss and safeguarding capital against adverse price movements.</p>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>The two metrics selected — <strong>Liquidity Depth Efficiency (<span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mtext>LDE</mtext><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">%</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\text{LDE}_{1\%}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.9024em;vertical-align:-0.2191em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">LDE</span></span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3448em;"><span style="top:-2.5198em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1%</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2191em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span>)</strong> and <strong>Capital Retention Ratio (CRR)</strong> — provide a concise, complementary view of a protocol-owned liquidity program:</p>

<thead>
<tr>
<th>Dimension</th>
<th>KPI</th>
<th>What it answers</th>
<th>Management value</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody><tr>
<td><strong>Upside (user benefit)</strong></td>
<td><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mtext>LDE</mtext><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">%</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\text{LDE}_{1\%}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.9024em;vertical-align:-0.2191em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">LDE</span></span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3448em;"><span style="top:-2.5198em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1%</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2191em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></td>
<td><em>&quot;How much low-slippage depth do end-users receive for every dollar we deploy?&quot;</em></td>
<td>Quantifies the pool&#39;s effectiveness as a market-making resource, directly tied to trading experience and on-chain volume.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td><strong>Downside (treasury cost)</strong></td>
<td><strong>CRR</strong></td>
<td><em>&quot;How costly to the protocol (through capital dilution) is the service provided to the end-users?&quot;</em></td>
<td>Isolates the economic drag of impermanent loss, signalling when range width, inventory controls, or rebalance cadence need adjustment.</td>
</tr>
</tbody></table>
<p>Together, <span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mtext>LDE</mtext><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">%</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\text{LDE}_{1\%}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.9024em;vertical-align:-0.2191em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">LDE</span></span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3448em;"><span style="top:-2.5198em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1%</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2191em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> and CRR form a balanced scorecard:</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mtext>LDE</mtext><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">%</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\text{LDE}_{1\%}</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height:0.9024em;vertical-align:-0.2191em;"></span><span class="mord"><span class="mord text"><span class="mord">LDE</span></span><span class="msupsub"><span class="vlist-t vlist-t2"><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.3448em;"><span style="top:-2.5198em;margin-right:0.05em;"><span class="pstrut" style="height:2.7em;"></span><span class="sizing reset-size6 size3 mtight"><span class="mord mtight"><span class="mord mtight">1%</span></span></span></span></span><span class="vlist-s">​</span></span><span class="vlist-r"><span class="vlist" style="height:0.2191em;"><span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span> tracks the <strong>utility</strong> delivered to traders.</li>
<li><strong>CRR</strong> tracks the <strong>cost</strong> borne by the protocol.</li>
</ul>
<p>Monitoring both KPIs side-by-side allows stakeholders to weigh the market-depth upside against the capital-preservation downside, enabling data-driven decisions on parameter tuning, deployment size, and overall strategy viability.</p>
